Inside every cell is a large, complex structure called the endoplasmic reticulum (ER). It is the cell's Amazon, sorting and shipping "packages" from the nucleus to everywhere they are needed. The ER relies on its complex shape to function properly, and malfunction results in Alzheimerâ€™s, diabetes, cardiac disease, and other illnesses. I develop tools to study how the ER's shape is maintained. In particular, I am building a correlative microscope that can image cellular structures at a resolution of 1/3000th of a human hair, and image analysis algorithms that link physical laws with subcellular membrane function. Currently, I do this in the Bewersdorf and Baddeley labs. Ask me about my research, cycling, climbing, or my cats.
